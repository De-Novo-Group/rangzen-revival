❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

General
What is Murmur?

Murmur is a delay-tolerant mesh messaging app. It lets people share short messages without internet access.

Messages spread person-to-person via Bluetooth and Wi-Fi when devices come near each other.

Is it anonymous?

YES

You can use any name (pseudonym) or none at all. The app does not link messages to your phone number, email, or device hardware ID.

Your "identity" is a cryptographic key generated on your phone.

Does it use the internet?

NO Messaging works entirely offline.

Note: If you enable "QA Mode" (for developers), it will use internet for telemetry. Do NOT enable in restricted environments.

Technical
How does it work offline?

When two Murmur users come near each other, their phones automatically exchange messages using:

  • Bluetooth (BLE) — Discovers nearby users and exchanges small amounts of data
  • Wi-Fi Direct — Transfers larger batches quickly (up to ~100m range)
  • Local Network — Works over shared Wi-Fi (cafes, hotspots)

No internet needed. Messages hop from phone to phone.

Why do I see a "Location" icon?

Android requires Location Permission to scan for Bluetooth and Wi-Fi devices. This is an Android rule, not ours.

Murmur does NOT track your GPS location or store it anywhere. We only use this permission to find nearby peers.

Security
Can the government see my messages?

NOT FROM A DISTANCE

There is no central server to hack or subpoena. Messages move directly from phone to phone — no company in the middle.

However: If someone (including a government agent) installs Murmur and physically comes near other users, their phone will receive messages like any other peer. This is unavoidable in any open mesh network.

Also: If someone takes your unlocked phone, they can read your messages. Use a strong screen lock.

Can I delete messages?

NO Messages cannot be deleted — they remain stored on your device.

You can hide messages by swiping them away. This clears your feed but doesn't erase anything. Use "Restore swiped" to bring them back.

Once a message spreads to others, it's out of your control. This is by design — it prevents censorship.

Are messages encrypted?

Messages are signed (to prove they came from the same author), but they are not encrypted — anyone on the mesh can read them.

Think of it like a public bulletin board, not a private chat. Do not share secrets.

Trust & Prioritization
How do I establish trust with someone?

The strongest way is to scan each other's QR codes in person. This creates a direct trust link.

Go to the Friends tab, show your QR code, and have them scan it (and vice versa). Once connected, messages from that person — and people they trust — will be prioritized in your feed.

You can also use "Hash Contacts" for weaker trust signals, but QR exchange is the gold standard.

Why does trust matter for message delivery?

When two phones meet, they only have a brief window to exchange messages — maybe seconds or minutes. Not everything can be shared in time.

Murmur must decide what to send first. It prioritizes messages from people in your trust network, so important content reaches you faster.

This means your feed naturally reflects your community. A government employee whose contacts are mostly other officials will see more official messages. A dissident whose trust circle includes activists will see more from that community — and less from outside it.

Trust shapes what spreads. Build your network intentionally.

Features
What does the Heart (❤️) do?

Tapping the heart is like voting up a message. It tells the network: "this is worth spreading."

Messages with more hearts are prioritized during exchanges — they spread further and faster.

Your identity is not attached to the heart. No one knows who voted.

What is the Trust indicator?

The colored bar on the left of each message shows how close the author is to your social circle:

  • Green — From a Friend or Friend-of-Friend
  • Yellow — Neutral (no strong connection)
  • Red — Unknown source (be skeptical)
What is "Hash Contacts"?

This helps Murmur figure out if you and another user share mutual contacts — without revealing who those contacts are.

  • Privacy: Your contacts never leave your phone
  • How: Phone numbers are scrambled into "hashes" (one-way codes) and compared locally
  • Result: Only the count of shared contacts is used, not identities

This is a weak trust signal. The strongest trust comes from scanning QR codes.

How do I share Murmur without internet?

Open Share Murmur from Settings. You have two options:

  • Quick Share / Bluetooth — Best for nearby devices. Tap "Share via Quick Share" and select how to send.
  • QR Code — If both devices are on the same WiFi, use the QR option. The receiver scans it with their camera and downloads via browser.

The receiver doesn't need Murmur installed — they accept the file and install it.
