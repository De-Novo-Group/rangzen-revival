❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

General
What is Murmur?

Murmur is a delay-tolerant mesh messaging app. It lets people share short messages without internet access.

Messages spread person-to-person via Bluetooth and Wi-Fi when devices come near each other.

Is it anonymous?

YES

You can use any name (pseudonym) or none at all. The app does not link messages to your phone number, email, or device hardware ID.

Your "identity" is a cryptographic key generated on your phone.

Does it use the internet?

NO Messaging works entirely offline.

Note: If you enable "QA Mode" (for developers), it will use internet for telemetry. Do NOT enable in restricted environments.

Features
What is "Import Contacts"?

This helps you find friends who also use Murmur.

  • Privacy: It does NOT upload your contacts
  • How it works: Phone numbers are "hashed" (scrambled) and compared with other Murmur users you meet
What does the Heart (❤️) do?

It's a local bookmark. Tapping the heart saves the message to your device.

Unlike social media, this "like" is NOT broadcast to the network. Other users don't see it.

What is the Trust indicator?

The colored bar on the left of each message:

  • Green — From a Friend or Friend-of-Friend
  • Yellow — Neutral
  • Red — Unknown source
Technical
How does it work offline?
  • Bluetooth (BLE) — Discover nearby users, exchange small data
  • Wi-Fi Direct — Move larger amounts quickly (~50-100m range)
  • LAN/Hotspot — Find peers on the same Wi-Fi network
Why do I see a "Location" icon?

Android requires Location Permission to scan for Bluetooth and Wi-Fi devices.

Murmur does NOT track your GPS location. We only use this permission to find nearby peers.

Security
Can the government see my messages?

NOT REMOTELY

There is no central server to hack or subpoena. Messages move directly from phone to phone.

Warning: If someone physically takes your unlocked phone, they can see your messages. Use a strong screen lock.

Can I delete a message?

You can delete it from your phone, but you cannot recall it from the network.

Once a message spreads, it's out of your control. This prevents censorship.
